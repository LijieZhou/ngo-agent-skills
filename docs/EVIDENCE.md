# Evidence Canon

The sourcing that grounds each skill in this library, organized by skill. This is a living document — add citations here as skills are built or revised, and pull each skill's `evidence_sources` frontmatter from this list rather than inventing sourcing per skill.

## Evidence tiers

Every skill's `evidence_strength` frontmatter field must be one of:

| Tier | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `established-standard` | Sourced from a recognized institutional standard the sector already defers to (OECD-DAC criteria, Sphere Standards, UN/DOSM official frameworks, donor-mandated formats like the logframe). |
| `evidence-based` | Sourced from peer-reviewed evaluation research demonstrating the approach works, independent of institutional adoption. |
| `emerging-practice` | Documented in credible sector guidance and used by practitioners, but without a strong empirical base yet. |
| `original-framework` | Our own design choice, explicitly labelled as such rather than presented as more authoritative than it is. |

## sdg-alignment-mapper

**Tier: `established-standard`**

- UN Statistics Division. *Global Indicator Framework for the Sustainable Development Goals and targets of the 2030 Agenda* (234 unique indicators post the March 2025 UNSC refinement). https://unstats.un.org/sdgs/
- Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM). *Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) Indicators, Malaysia, 2024.*
- Economic Planning Unit, Malaysia. *SDG Roadmap for Malaysia Phase II: 2021-2025.*

## logframe-toc-builder

**Tier: `established-standard`**

- NORAD. *The Logical Framework Approach (LFA): Handbook for Objectives-Oriented Planning*, 4th ed., 1999 — the logframe format required by most bilateral and multilateral donors.
- USAID. *ADS Chapter 201: Program Cycle Operational Policy* — results frameworks and logframe requirements for USAID-funded programs.
- Vogel, I. (2012). *Review of the Use of "Theory of Change" in International Development.* UK Department for International Development (DFID).
- Mayne, J. (2015). Useful Theory of Change models. *Canadian Journal of Program Evaluation, 30*(2), 119-142.
